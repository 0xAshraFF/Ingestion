# Page 4 - H. L. A. Hart

Hart believed that morality had an influence on law, but very rarely. For that he used the term minimum content of natural law.

Hart said in his book The Concept of Law that law is an obligation, but natural law is not an obligation.

Hart disagreed with Austin's theory that law is a command.

Reasons Hart disagreed:

1. All laws are not commands, and not all laws are backed by sanctions.
2. Sometimes laws confer powers, for example fundamental rights.
3. Sometimes the result of not following laws makes a contract void, but sanctions or punishment are not present.
4. Customs are not always law, for example Islamic customs in Muslim society.
5. There is no continuity of a particular sovereign, because one can die or retire.
6. Sovereigns have no unlimited power; there are limited powers.

Hart said there are two types of rules:

1. Primary rules: rules that give benefits and duties.
2. Secondary rules: rules through which we can add new rules to the previous legal system.
