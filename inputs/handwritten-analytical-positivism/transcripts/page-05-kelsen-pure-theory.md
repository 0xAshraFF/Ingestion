# Page 5 - Hans Kelsen

Kelsen gave the Pure Theory of Law to identify the essence of law and how laws are being made.

Kelsen interpreted law in a different way. He said laws are neither commands nor obligations; they are directions.

These are official norms or directions given to officials to follow.

One norm is related to another norm and finally comes to the grundnorm, or fundamental norm. The Indian Constitution is an example of a grundnorm because it gives validity to all directions and laws.

Kelsen is known as doubly pure because he talked neither about morality nor about any other concept. He said what law is and did not mention the origin and repercussions.
