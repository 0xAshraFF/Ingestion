# Page 2 - Austin, Positive Law, and Positive Morality

Austin said that customs become law after judicial recognition.

Austin did not consider what law ought to be. He only considered what law is in the present time.

Austin differentiated between positive law and positive morality.

## Positive Law

Law strictly so called: laws are commands backed by sanctions.

These laws are set by men as political superiors to their inferiors.

Laws are set by men in pursuance of political rights. Example: I am saving my property or selling it because this right is given by the state.

## Positive Morality

Laws are not strictly so called. These rules are working in society, but Austin did not accept them as law.

Laws by analogy: these are not laws, but people respect these obligations. Examples include avoiding eating in a masjid or avoiding use of a cell phone in a masjid.

## Jeremy Bentham

Sovereigns, big state institutions, persons, and assemblies of persons have powers distributed among them.

Bentham divided jurisprudence into two parts:

1. Expository: what law is.
2. Censorial: what law ought to be.

Bentham opposed the idea of natural law and natural rights.
