## Known facts

1. Analytical positivism focuses on what law is, not what law ought to be.
2. John Austin is presented as the founder of the Analytical School of Jurisprudence and associated with command or imperative theory.
3. Austin distinguishes positive law from positive morality. Positive law is command backed by sanction; positive morality includes social rules or obligations not treated as law.
4. Jeremy Bentham divides jurisprudence into expository jurisprudence and censorial jurisprudence.
5. Bentham is associated with utilitarianism, maximum pleasure, hedonism, and the seven-factor felicific calculus.
6. Hart disagrees with Austin's command theory and argues that law includes rules that confer powers, not only sanction-backed commands.
7. Hart distinguishes primary rules from secondary rules.
8. Kelsen's Pure Theory treats law as a system of norms or directions, ultimately connected to a grundnorm.
