# Page 1 - Analytical Positivism

Analytical School cares about "what law is" and not "what the law ought to be."

Analytical School is also called the Positivist School because it is based on positivism.

Morals are not objective. Law needs to be objective, but if morals are added to the definition of law, law will not be objective anymore.

The word positivism comes from the word "positum," which means "as it is."

As per positivism, laws are commands. This is why this school is also called the Imperative School of Jurisprudence, because imperative means commands of sovereign.

As per positivism, analysis of legal concepts can be done without involving sociological, historical, and moral aspects.

In an ideal world, it is important to see what the laws should be, but in the contemporary era it is important to see what the law is.

## John Austin

Book: The Province of Jurisprudence.

Austin was an English jurist and founder of the Analytical School of Jurisprudence.

Austin thought that natural law is inalienable, but without state these natural laws are incomplete or impossible because rights are given by the state.

Austin gave the command theory of law, also called imperative theory, which means laws are commands and backed by sanctions.
