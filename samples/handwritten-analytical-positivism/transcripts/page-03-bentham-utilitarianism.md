# Page 3 - Bentham and Utilitarianism

Natural law was called nonsense and anarchic fallacies by Bentham.

Bentham is famous for the concept of utilitarianism, which means maximum pleasure to most people and is based on the concept of hedonism.

Pleasure can be quantified by the utilitarian, felicific, or hedonistic calculus, which consists of seven factors:

1. Intensity: how strong the pleasure is.
2. Duration: how long the pleasure will last.
3. Certainty or uncertainty: whether pleasure may or may not occur.
4. Remoteness: how soon the pleasure will occur.
5. Purity: whether there will be any sensation opposite to pleasure.
6. Extent: how many people will be affected.
7. Fecundity: the probability that an action will be followed by sensations of the same kind.

Bentham first used the term international law.

Bentham wrote the book A Fragment on Government.
